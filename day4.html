TAJ MAHAL


Name Location Built by Construction Period Purpose
Taj Mahal Agra,India Shah jahan 1632 – 1653 Mausoleum for Mumtaz Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, commissioned by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1631 to house the tomb of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and also his own tomb. Considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a symbol of love, its construction was completed in 1648. The complex includes the main mausoleum, red sandstone buildings, and a "charbagh" Persian-style garden.

History of Construction

The inspiration for the Taj Mahal was a verse by Bibadal Khan, the imperial goldsmith and poet. The vision behind the construction was to make an earthly replica of Mumtaz Mahal’s celestial abode in paradise. The plans for the complex have been attributed to various architects of the period, though the chief architect was probably Ustad Aḥmad Lahawrī, an Indian of Persian descent. The five principal elements of the complex—main gateway, garden, mosque, jawāb (literally “answer”; a building mirroring the mosque), and mausoleum (including its four minarets)—were conceived and designed as a unified entity according to the tenets of Mughal building practice, which allowed no subsequent addition or alteration. Building commenced about 1632. More than 20,000 workers were employed from India, Persia, the Ottoman Empire, and Europe to complete the mausoleum itself by about 1638–39; the adjunct buildings were finished by 1643, and decoration work continued until at least 1647. In total, construction of the 42-acre (17-hectare) complex spanned 22 years.

Key Facts

  1. Purpose: It was commissioned as a mausoleum and is often called a testament to love, holding the remains of both Mumtaz Mahal and, later, Shah Jahan.
  2. Architecture and design: The structure is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles. It is famous for its white marble, intricate carvings, and detailed calligraphy.
  3. Construction: Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, involving approximately 20,000 workers and over 1,000 elephants to transport materials.
  4. Location: It is situated on the bank of the Yamuna River in the city of Agra, India.
  5. Recognition: The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Agra Fort Agra,India Akbar 1565 – 1573 Military fort and royal residence

The Agra Fort (Qila Agra) is a historical Mughal fort in the city of Agra, also known as Agra's Red Fort. Mughal emperor Humayun was crowned at this fort in 1530. It was later renovated by the Mughal emperor Akbar from 1565 and the present-day structure was completed in 1573. It served as the main residence of the rulers of the Mughal dynasty until 1638, when the capital was shifted from Agra to Delhi. It was also known as the "Lal-Qila" or "Qila-i-Akbari".[1] Before being captured by the British, the last Indian rulers to have occupied it were the Marathas. In 1983, the Agra fort was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site because of its importance during Mughal rule.[2] It is about 2.5 kilometers (1.6 mi) northwest of its more famous sister monument, the Taj Mahal. The fort can be more accurately described as a walled city. It was later renovated by Shah Jahan.

History

After the First Battle of Panipat in 1526, Babur stayed in the fort, in the palace of Ibrahim Lodi. He later built a baoli (step well) in it. His successor, Humayun, was crowned in the fort in 1530. He was defeated at Bilgram in 1540 by Sher Shah Suri. The fort remained with the Suris till 1555, when Humayun recaptured it. His historian, Abul Fazl, recorded that this was a brick fort known as 'Badalgarh'. It was in a ruined condition and Akbar had it rebuilt with red sandstone from Barauli area Dhaulpur district, in Rajasthan.[4] Architects laid the foundation and it was built with bricks in the inner core with sandstone on external surfaces. Some 4,000 builders worked on it daily for eight years, completing it in 1573.[5][6]

Architecture Feature

  • Shape: A semi-circular fort with massive circular bastions.
  • Walls: Features a 21.4-meter high fortification wall and is surrounded by a moat.
  • Gates: Has four gates, though only the Amar Singh Gate is open to the public.

RED FORT


Name Location Built by Construction Period Purpose
Red Fort Delhi,India Shah jahan 1638 – 1648 Royal residence and administrative center

The Red Fort is a historic fort in Delhi, India, built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-1600s, which served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly two centuries. Constructed from red sandstone, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major landmark of Mughal architecture, housing palaces, halls, gardens, and a mosque. Today, it is a major tourist attraction and the site of the annual Independence Day flag-hoisting ceremony.

History

The Red Fort's history began with its construction by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639 to serve as the new palace for his capital, Shahjahanabad (now Old Delhi). Completed in 1648, the fort was the primary residence and political center for the Mughal emperors for nearly two centuries. Named for its massive red sandstone walls, it became a symbol of Mughal power and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. After India's independence in 1947, it has been the site where the prime minister hoists the national flag annually on August 15th.

Key feature

Massive Walls:
Enclosed by imposing red sandstone walls, the fort stretches over 2.5 km and rises to a height of about 33 meters, with a moat for protection.
Main Gates:
The primary entry points are the Lahore Gate and the Delhi Gate. The Lahore Gate is especially significant as the location where the Prime Minister hoists the Indian flag on Independence Day.
Layout:
The fort is an irregular octagon, covering an area of 125 acres.
